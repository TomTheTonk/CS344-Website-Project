Our Approach to Outsourcing

What is Outsourcing?

Outsourcing is the practice of hiring an outside company or individual(s) to perform services or manufacture products, often to free up in-house employees for higher-level tasks or to cut equipment overhead costs. This can cut costs and serve as a strategic management tool for businesses.

Advantages and Disadvantages

  • Outsourcing can help companies benefit from the differences in labor and production costs in other countries, allowing the business to increase profits while staying competitive in the industry.

  • When one outsources, one has access to a much larger variety of employees with different skills. If one needs specialized labor, outsourcing is a great option.

  • Hiring people around the world in different timezones means that, with coordination, one can create a 24-hour production cycle. This means that production, IT, customer support, etc. can keep running at all times.

  • Writing and signing contracts with outside companies may take time and effort for a firm's legal team and must be thorough in order to prevent possible legal liabilities.

  • One must be cautious to avoid data breaches due to outside parties having access to one's company's private data.

  • Outsourcing has some ethical criticisms: contract workers, especially in other countries, might earn less money and fewer benefits, creating workforce inequality.

  • Communication with people in other countries and timezones can be difficult.

Our Approach

At Advanced Manufacturing, our approach to outsourcing follows the same values that built our company from a five-person team to a large company with roots around the globe. We partner with skilled individuals and teams around the world to improve our efficiency and productivity as we design and manufacture more great products. Every decision we make is based in our commitment to quality and collaboration with the people who are integral to our company's success.
